# Use Cases

## 1. Literature review and evidence synthesis

### What the researcher is trying to do

- define a review question
- collect candidate literature
- verify formal references
- turn the result into a usable synthesis

### What the workspace should make visible

- which project this review belongs to
- which formal references are ready for use
- what the next step is after discovery
- whether the work should stay simple or expand into a research squad

### Why this workspace helps

It does not stop at discovery. It keeps the literature process connected to project state, source boundaries, later writing, and review readiness.

## 2. Social-platform case study

### What the researcher is trying to do

- read browser-visible platform content
- record what was actually observed
- separate capture from interpretation
- prepare material for coding and later comparison

### What the workspace should make visible

- source provenance
- platform and permission boundaries
- evidence entries for the current project
- whether the case should become a dataset or a report

### Why this workspace helps

It treats social evidence as research material, not just as “web scraping”.

## 3. Computational social science project

### What the researcher is trying to do

- combine literature, material collection, analysis, writing, and reproducibility
- keep the project inspectable across multiple stages

### What the workspace should make visible

- current stage
- current owner
- blockers and milestones
- active gates
- writing and submission readiness

### Why this workspace helps

It keeps the project legible as a system instead of collapsing it into one thread or one notebook.

## 4. Writing, revision, and submission

### What the researcher is trying to do

- draft a manuscript
- verify references before formal use
- review writing quality
- assemble response materials and final submission assets

### What the workspace should make visible

- current draft stage
- writing checks
- reviewer links
- final package readiness

### Why this workspace helps

It treats writing as the downstream result of source and analysis work, not as a detached export step.
