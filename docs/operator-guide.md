# Operator Guide

VELA is operated as a research workflow environment. The operator's job is to keep the project state truthful enough for Codex, HELM, or a human collaborator to inspect without guessing.

## Basic Operating Loop

1. Define or open the project.
2. Capture materials before evaluating them.
3. Upgrade only verified materials into evidence.
4. Record candidate claims separately from supported claims.
5. Write method notes and known assumptions.
6. Prepare bounded Codex handoffs.
7. Review outputs before they alter project facts.
8. Export or share only the state the evidence can support.

## Quality Checks

Quality checks should answer practical questions:

- Is the research question present?
- Are materials sourceable?
- Does evidence include access time, verification status, and rights or ethics notes?
- Does each supported claim have linked evidence?
- Does the evidence actually support the claim as written?
- Are method assumptions visible?
- Is the Codex handoff scoped enough to avoid accidental overreach?

## Source Of Truth

VELA should keep the source of truth in portable project files. HELM may project those files into a local board, but the board should not be the only place where the research record exists.
